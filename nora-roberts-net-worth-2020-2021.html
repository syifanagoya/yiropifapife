Early Life & Biography

Nora Roberts was born in Maryland. She is the youngest of all her siblings. As the family was comprised of passionate readers, she inclined reading and writing from childhood days. She had formed a strong bond with writing in her formative years and took her passion as her career.

When she was young, she used to write stories in her notebook. As she grew, she wrote various manuscripts, that ended up with rejection. She came with her first publication in 1981 as “Irish Thoroughbred.”

Personal Life

In 1968, Nora got married to Ronald Aufdem Brinke after her graduation. The couple gave birth to two sons, Dan and Jason. At that time, she was a homemaker and remained at home with her sons. She spent time doing crafts that included sewing the clothes of her children and ceramic jobs.

Unfortunately, their marriage did not go well. The couple divorced in 1983. Later, she married Bruce Wilder in July 1985. It was the time when she was matured and have pursued her career. He assisted her in building her bookstore. They expanded their home, as well as traveled the world together.

Career

Post schooling, she got her first writing opportunity in February 1979, during a blizzard. In 1981, she published her first novel, Irish Thoroughbred. She got real recognition from romance readers in 1985 from her bestselling romantic series, “Playing the Odds.” It was an instant success.

Between 1999 and 2000, Nora had written several more novels that got listed under the “best selling romantic novels” category by USA Today. Her two popular novels, ‘Magic Moments’ and ‘Sanctuary,’ got even converted to movies. Nora Roberts had suffered from plagiarism by the writer Janet Dailey.
