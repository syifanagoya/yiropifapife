Biography

Bernard Bragg was born in New York on September 27, 1928. American performer, artist, and writer who founded The National Theater of the Deaf and was called the leading professional deaf actor in the country by the New York Times. He studied mime with Marcel Marceau, whom he met in 1956. He went to New York School for the Deaf and performed in local theatre.
