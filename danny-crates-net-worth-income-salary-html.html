Danny Crates (born 9 February 1973) is a former British sprinter who specialised in the 800 metres. In this event, he holds the Paralympic world record and has won gold medals in many international events, including the Paralympic Games. He’s also a certified diving instructor and a competitive rugby player. Crates was born in the Essex town of Orsett. After losing his right arm in a vehicle accident in Australia in 1994, he turned to sports. He participated in T46 (arm amputee) sports and won bronze in the 400m sprint at the 2000 Summer Paralympics in Sydney, and gold in the 800m at the 2004 Summer Paralympics in Athens. Crates moved from the 400m to the 800m after Sydney, where he broke the world record with a run of 1:53.27 at the AAA Championships in 2004.  Crates has previously won gold at the European Championships in the 800m event in 2003 and 200In 2003, he won gold in the same event at the World Indoor Championships. In 2001, he had won gold in the 400m at the European Championships. In 2004, he won gold in the 800m at the first Paralympic World Cup in Manchester, however he did not participate in 2008 to defend his title. He also participates in competitive rugby with non-amputee teammates. In 2008, Crates carried the Olympic torch in London and participated in the Beijing Summer Paralympics. After participating in the London Grand Prix at Crystal Palace on July 25, 2009, Crates announced his retirement from athletics. Crates finished third in his last race and earned 11 gold and one bronze medal throughout his career. He was a member of the UK Channel 4 commentary crew at the 2012 Paralympic Games in London. Danny Boy is the title of his book, which was released in 2012.
