These comparisons highlight Menzel’s significant financial success, largely attributed to her diverse roles on Broadway and in major films like Frozen.

Career Achievements and Their Impact on Net Worth

Menzel’s journey to financial success began with her Broadway debut in Rent in 1996. Playing Maureen Johnson not only earned her a Tony Award nomination but also put her on the map as a rising star.

She then cemented her status with her role as Elphaba in Wicked, which won her a Tony Award for Best Leading Actress in a Musical. These roles significantly boosted her earnings and contributed to her growing net worth.

Her transition to film and television also played a crucial role. Voicing Elsa in Frozen and singing the hit song Let It Go skyrocketed her fame and financial status.

The success of Frozen, which grossed over $1.28 billion, translated into significant earnings for Menzel. Other notable screen appearances include Enchanted, Glee, and Uncut Gems.
