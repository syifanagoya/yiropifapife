Susie Essman Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Background

From her early days in The Bronx, Susie Essman’s life was destined to be anything but ordinary. Born to Leonard and Zora Essman, she grew up imbued with a rich cultural heritage that would later seep into her comedy, making her one of the industry’s funniest stand-up talents.

After honing her craft at SUNY Purchase College, Susie embarked on a journey through the comedy circuits of New York, eventually making waves with her vibrant stand-up routines that often drew from her ethnic background and personal experiences.

Breakthrough in Comedy

Her breakthrough came when she graced The Tonight Show stage in 1989, a moment that catapulted her into the comedy stratosphere. But it was her role as the feisty Susie Greene in Curb Your Enthusiasm that truly made her a household name.

With her character’s sharp wit and memorable catchphrases, Susie became an icon of humor and relatability.

Versatility and Legacy

Beyond the laughs, Susie’s voice brought life to Mittens the cat in Disney’s Bolt, showcasing her versatility and reaching audiences beyond the stand-up scene.

Her appearances across various media, including the roast of Bob Saget and her book What Would Susie Say, further cement her legacy as a multifaceted entertainer.

FAQs about Susie Essman

Who is Susie Essman?

Susie Essman is an American stand-up comedian, actress, and writer, widely recognized for her role as Susie Greene on the HBO comedy series Curb Your Enthusiasm.

What is she known for?

Essman is best known for her portrayal of Susie Greene, the wife of Jeff Greene (played by Jeff Garlin), in the critically acclaimed HBO series Curb Your Enthusiasm. She has also lent her voice to characters in animated movies such as Bolt (2008).

When was she born?

She was born on May 31, 1955, in Mount Vernon, New York, USA.

Is she married?

Yes, she has been married to Jim Harder since September 13, 2008.

Does she have children?

While she does not have biological children, she became a stepmother to four children upon marrying Jim Harder. She also has a Pomeranian, which humorously complements her family alongside Jim’s Shih Tzu.

Where did Susie Essman go to college?

She received her bachelor’s degree from SUNY Purchase College.

Has she written any books?

Yes, she authored What Would Susie Say?: Bullsh*t Wisdom About Love, Life, and Comedy, offering insights and humorous takes on various aspects of life.

What awards has she been nominated for?

She has received nominations for several awards, including the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Ensemble in a Comedy Series and the Critics’ Choice Television Award for Best Guest Performer in a Comedy Series.

What other TV shows and movies has Susie Essman appeared in?

Beyond Curb Your Enthusiasm, Essman has appeared in Hacks, Bless This Mess, Broad City, Those Who Can’t, and Law & Order: SVU. She also voiced Mittens the Cat in Bolt and Esther Lonstein in American Dad.

How did she start her career?

She began her acting career with small parts in films like Crocodile Dundee II and Punchline in 1988 before achieving fame with her role in Curb Your Enthusiasm.
